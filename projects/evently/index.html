Evently

An event discovery platform designed to make finding and hosting experiences more joyful, personalized, and efficient.

Web App Mobile App Discovery Product Design

Evently is an event discovery platform designed to make finding and hosting experiences more joyful, personalized, and efficient for two key personas: Explorers and Creators / Hosts.

The platform focuses on improving discovery through personalization, intuitive filters, and creator-focused tools, while making the overall experience feel more engaging and human.

Challenges

Where It Hurt

Overwhelming discovery

Users often face endless event listings without meaningful personalization, making it hard to surface what’s relevant.

Limited filtering

Most platforms rely on basic filters like location and date, making it difficult to narrow down relevant events.

Decision fatigue

With too many options and little guidance, users struggle to choose events confidently and abandon the search.

Low visibility for creators

Hosts often rely on social media promotion to reach audiences, with few built-in tools to grow their community.

Communication gap

Users cannot easily ask questions or interact with hosts before booking, reducing trust and commitment.

Lack of emotional engagement

Existing platforms feel transactional rather than inspiring, missing the excitement that drives attendance.

Research

Dual Perspectives

To understand both sides of the platform, qualitative user research was conducted with two user groups: event explorers and event hosts.

User Interviews

Each focus area is paired with the insight it directly uncovered, creating a clear thread from question to discovery.

How users discover events

Recommendations from friends are often more trusted than algorithmic platform suggestions.

What excites them about attending

Discovery needs to feel personal, generic category lists feel cold and transactional.

Pain points when browsing event platforms

Users feel overwhelmed by long event listings and want smarter suggestions instead of manual browsing.

Challenges hosts face while promoting events

Hosts struggle with event visibility and promotion, relying on external channels to reach audiences.

Empathy Mapping

Distilling emotional and behavioral patterns into two clear archetypes to guide design decisions.

Explorer

”I feel overwhelmed with too many options and nothing feels right.”

Feels overwhelmed with too many event options, often abandons searches due to decision fatigue, and wants recommendations that feel trustworthy and relevant.

Host

”I spend more time promoting than planning my events.”

Finds promotion time-consuming and fragmented across channels, worries about attendance and cancellations, and needs simple tools to manage events and reach audiences.

Competitive Observation

Many existing event platforms focus heavily on ticket transactions rather than experience discovery.

The competitive landscape revealed three common gaps that create friction for both explorers and hosts.

Limited personalization, one-size-fits-all lists

Overreliance on long event lists without smart filtering

Niche interests buried under generic listings

Key Learnings

Lessons in Discovery

Emotion matters

Experiences should feel exciting, not purely functional. Emotional engagement drives discovery and loyalty.

Reduce cognitive load

Discovery products must not force users to scroll endlessly. Smart filtering and personalization are essential.

Balance both sides

Designing for two user groups requires careful balance, explorers want inspiration, while creators want control and visibility.

Reflection

Evently reinforced how thoughtful UX design can transform everyday tasks like event discovery into meaningful experiences. By designing for two distinct user groups, explorers seeking inspiration and creators seeking visibility, the project highlighted the importance of balancing emotional engagement with practical utility. The biggest takeaway was that reducing cognitive load through smarter personalization and filtering is not just a convenience, but the core of a joyful discovery experience.

GuruKool is an all-in-one platform designed to bridge the gap between home and school. We helped GuruKool Inc. design and build a mobile experience that empowers parents to stay involved in their children’s education, students to manage their studies independently, and educators to communicate effectively with families.

The Problem

Problem Statement Visual

Parents today juggle multiple apps, group chats, and paper notes to keep track of their children’s homework and school activities. Fragmented communication between teachers and families leads to missed assignments, confusion about expectations, and unnecessary stress for both parents and students.

“I check three different apps every evening just to figure out what my kids need to do for homework. It’s exhausting.” — Parent, User Research Participant

Key pain points identified through early stakeholder interviews:

  • No centralized place for homework tracking across multiple children and schools
  • Teachers rely on one-way communication channels like newsletters and emails that go unread
  • Students lack tools to develop independent study habits and time management skills
  • Parents feel disconnected from the classroom and unsure how to help effectively
  • Group study and peer support happen informally with no structured platform

Research Insights

We conducted 40+ user interviews across three personas — parents, students (ages 10–17), and teachers — along with a quantitative survey of 600 respondents. The research phase uncovered critical insights that shaped our design decisions.

1

Parents Want Visibility, Not Control

Parents don’t want to micromanage homework. They want a clear view of what’s due, what’s completed, and where their child needs help — without hovering.

2

Students Crave Peer Support

78% of students said they learn best when working with peers. Study groups — even informal ones — significantly improve comprehension and motivation.

3

Teachers Need Two-Way Channels

Teachers reported spending 5+ hours per week on parent communication. They wanted structured, asynchronous touchpoints rather than ad-hoc messages.

The Solution

GuruKool brings homework management, community, and communication into a single, beautifully designed app. Here are the core features we built:

Feature 01

Homework Tracker

A unified dashboard where students see all assignments organized by subject, due date, and priority. Smart reminders nudge students before deadlines, and completion streaks gamify consistency. Parents get a read-only view with daily digest notifications.

Homework Tracker Screen
Study Groups Screen
Feature 02

Study Groups

Students can create or join study groups by subject, school, or topic. Groups include shared resource boards, a discussion thread, and a simple quiz tool so members can test each other. Moderation tools keep the space safe and focused.

Feature 03

Parent Dashboard

A bird’s-eye view of each child’s academic life — upcoming assignments, grades, attendance, and teacher notes. Parents can set goals, track progress over time, and celebrate milestones. Multi-child support lets families manage everything in one place.

Parent Dashboard Screen
Teacher Communication Screen
Feature 04

Teacher Communication

Teachers send announcements, assignment details, and progress updates directly through the app. A templated message system saves time, while read receipts ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Parents can reply in-thread to keep conversations organized.

Iteration Highlights

  • v1

    Homework as a list. Initial designs used a simple checklist. Users found it overwhelming when they had 15+ assignments visible at once.

  • v2

    Grouped by subject with collapsible sections. This reduced cognitive load significantly. Users could focus on one subject at a time while still having full visibility.

  • v3

    Added a “Today” view. User testing revealed that students primarily care about what’s due today and tomorrow. A prioritized daily view became the default landing screen.

  • v4

    Streak system and micro-animations. Gamification elements increased daily active usage by 34% during beta testing.

User Testing

We ran three rounds of usability testing with 12 participants per round, covering all three personas. Each round informed critical refinements.

Round 1: Navigation Confusion

Users struggled to find the study groups feature. We moved it from a buried tab to a prominent bottom nav item, increasing discovery by 60%.

Round 2: Notification Overload

Parents felt overwhelmed by per-assignment notifications. We introduced daily digest options and granular notification preferences.

Round 3: Onboarding Drop-off

Students abandoned setup halfway through. We reduced onboarding to 3 screens with progressive profiling, cutting drop-off by 45%.

Round 3: Accessibility Gaps

Color contrast on status indicators failed WCAG AA. We redesigned the entire status system with both color and iconography for clarity.

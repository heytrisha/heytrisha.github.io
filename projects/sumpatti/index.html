Sampatti [WIP]

Designing for the moment before the first investment, not after it

Product design Fintech India market User research Information architecture

Hero Image — Sampatti App Overview

“Every major Indian investment app is designed for the moment after the decision to invest. Sampatti designs for the moment before it.”

40 million first-time investors entered the Indian market between 2020 and 2023. The apps are polished, the intent is real, and the infrastructure exists. Yet most of them never make their first investment. This case study is about why — and what happens when you design for the user who is still deciding.

02 · Segmentation & Focus

Four segments. One deliberate focus.

Mapping the full landscape before narrowing — to show why the choice matters

1Not our user

The financially unaware

No working knowledge of financial concepts. Has not Googled SIPs or watched finance content. Needs foundational literacy before any investment tool is relevant.

2Adjacent — not our focus

The curious but conceptually shallow

Has heard of SIPs and mutual funds socially. Watched a few reels. Knows the vocabulary but not the logic behind it. Has never opened an investment app.

Designing for them would recreate an educational product — a crowded space already served by Varsity, Finology, and others.

3Our segment

The aware but inactive

Understands personal finance concepts. Has watched finance content, read articles, and researched platforms. Has downloaded an investment app and completed KYC — but has never made a single investment. Stuck at the decision threshold, not the knowledge threshold.

4Not our user

The active investor

Already has active SIPs or stock positions. Comparing fund performance, tracking XIRR, managing a portfolio. Well served by Zerodha, ET Money, and INDmoney.

Segment 3 was chosen because it has the clearest unmet need, the most designable problem, and the strongest business case. Users at this point have money and intent — the shortest conversion path — and need personalised guidance, not overwhelming options.

04 · Product Philosophy

Six principles that shape every decision

Every Indian investment app is built for someone who has already decided to invest. Sampatti is built for someone who is still deciding.

Principle 01

One recommendation, not a catalogue

Groww shows you 200 funds. Zerodha gives you a search bar. ET Money runs a quiz and hands you a list. All of them assume you know what to pick. Sampatti assumes you don’t. Two questions — your financial situation and how you react to risk — produce a personalised portfolio split. Debt and equity, in a ratio that makes sense for you. One clear starting point, with the reasoning visible. No scrolling. No comparing. No guessing.

Principle 02

Trust before the money screen

On most platforms, SEBI registration is in the footer. Fund house details are behind a link. Withdrawal terms are in terms and conditions nobody reads. Sampatti shows these where they matter — in the flow, before the payment screen. Where your money goes. Which fund house holds it. What SEBI registration means. When you can withdraw. By the time you enter your PIN, you have already seen five trust signals in the right order, at the right moment.

Principle 03

The first step should feel reversible

Most investment apps frame the first SIP as a serious financial commitment. The language is formal. The amounts feel permanent. Sampatti does the opposite. Start with ₹100. Pause anytime. Stop anytime. The biggest barrier to a first investment is not the money — it is the feeling that you are making a decision you cannot undo. Sampatti frames it as a trial, not a commitment. You are not signing a contract. You are trying something.

Principle 04

Explain things where they matter, not in a glossary

”Exit load: 1% if redeemed before 1 year.” Every app shows this. None of them explain what it means for you. Sampatti does. One tap, and you see the answer — ₹5 on your ₹500 SIP, in plain language. Collapsible, inline, gone after you have read it. The information lives exactly where the question lives. Not in a separate FAQ. Not in a blog post you’ll never find. Right there, when you need it, and nowhere else.

Principle 05

Your numbers, not generic projections

Every platform shows compounding calculators. Enter ₹5,000, select 10 years, see a number. That number feels like it belongs to someone else. Sampatti calculates from your income, your age, and your allocation. “₹3,000 per month starting at 23 could become ₹38 lakh by 40.” That is not a marketing illustration. It is your number. When the projection is personal, the future stops being abstract. That is the moment investing stops feeling like something other people do.

Principle 06

Guidance that learns, not a one-time recommendation

On most platforms, the experience ends the moment you start a SIP. You get a confirmation screen, and then you are on your own. Sampatti treats the first investment as the beginning, not the end. As you invest consistently, the system adapts. Your portfolio grows with you — from first SIP to multiple goals. Recommendations evolve based on your behaviour, not a static plan you set once and forget. Other apps help you start. Sampatti helps you continue.

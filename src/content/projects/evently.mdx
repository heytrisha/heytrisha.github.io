  {/* Design Approach */}
  <div class="mb-16">
    <h2 class="text-sm uppercase tracking-widest text-gray-500 mb-4">Design Approach</h2>
    <h3 class="text-3xl md:text-4xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-6 leading-tight">
      Discovery, not search.
    </h3>
    <p class="text-gray-400 text-lg leading-relaxed mb-8">
      We designed Evently around three core principles: spatial discovery through maps, mood-based filtering that mirrors how people actually think about plans, and social proof that builds confidence to attend something new.
    </p>

    <div class="space-y-8">
      <div class="flex items-start gap-6">
        <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/[0.05] border border-white/10 flex items-center justify-center text-[#fafafa] font-bold text-sm shrink-0 mt-1">
          01
        </div>
        <div>
          <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-[#fafafa] mb-2">Map-First Discovery</h4>
          <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
            The default view is an interactive map, not a list. People think spatially — "what is happening near me" or "what is happening near the restaurant I have a reservation at." The map lets users drop pins, draw boundaries, and explore neighborhoods organically.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="flex items-start gap-6">
        <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/[0.05] border border-white/10 flex items-center justify-center text-[#fafafa] font-bold text-sm shrink-0 mt-1">
          02
        </div>
        <div>
          <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-[#fafafa] mb-2">Mood-Based Filtering</h4>
          <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
            Instead of rigid categories, users can filter by mood and intent: "I want to learn something," "I want to be outdoors," "I want a low-key evening," "I want high energy." This maps to how people actually decide what to do — by feeling first, category second.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="flex items-start gap-6">
        <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/[0.05] border border-white/10 flex items-center justify-center text-[#fafafa] font-bold text-sm shrink-0 mt-1">
          03
        </div>
        <div>
          <h4 class="text-xl font-semibold text-[#fafafa] mb-2">Social Proof Layer</h4>
          <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
            Every event surface shows how many people are interested, which friends have saved it, and community ratings. For first-time attendees trying something new — a salsa class or an open mic — this social signal reduces the anxiety of showing up alone.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  {/* Reflection */}
  <div class="mb-8">
    <h2 class="text-sm uppercase tracking-widest text-gray-500 mb-4">Reflection</h2>
    <p class="text-gray-400 text-lg leading-relaxed">
      Evently validated a core hypothesis: people do not want more events — they want better discovery. By designing for intent and mood rather than rigid categories, and by layering social context over every surface, we turned a utility into a habit. The biggest learning was that map-first discovery works, but only when paired with strong filtering — without it, the map becomes overwhelming. Mood-based filters became the bridge between spatial exploration and personal relevance.
    </p>

{/* 04 · Product Philosophy */}
<section className="max-w-6xl mx-auto px-6 py-20">
  <span className="text-sm font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">04 · Product Philosophy</span>
  <h2 className="text-4xl font-bold mt-4 mb-4">Six principles that shape every decision</h2>
  <p className="text-gray-400 text-lg mb-12">Every Indian investment app is built for someone who has already decided to invest. Sampatti is built for someone who is still deciding.</p>

  {/* Principle 01 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 01</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">One recommendation, not a catalogue</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">Groww shows you 200 funds. Zerodha gives you a search bar. ET Money runs a quiz and hands you a list. All of them assume you know what to pick. Sampatti assumes you don't. Two questions — your financial situation and how you react to risk — produce a personalised portfolio split. Debt and equity, in a ratio that makes sense for you. One clear starting point, with the reasoning visible. No scrolling. No comparing. No guessing.</p>
  </div>

  {/* Principle 02 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 02</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">Trust before the money screen</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">On most platforms, SEBI registration is in the footer. Fund house details are behind a link. Withdrawal terms are in terms and conditions nobody reads. Sampatti shows these where they matter — in the flow, before the payment screen. Where your money goes. Which fund house holds it. What SEBI registration means. When you can withdraw. By the time you enter your PIN, you have already seen five trust signals in the right order, at the right moment.</p>
  </div>

  {/* Principle 03 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 03</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">The first step should feel reversible</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">Most investment apps frame the first SIP as a serious financial commitment. The language is formal. The amounts feel permanent. Sampatti does the opposite. Start with ₹100. Pause anytime. Stop anytime. The biggest barrier to a first investment is not the money — it is the feeling that you are making a decision you cannot undo. Sampatti frames it as a trial, not a commitment. You are not signing a contract. You are trying something.</p>
  </div>

  {/* Principle 04 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 04</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">Explain things where they matter, not in a glossary</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">"Exit load: 1% if redeemed before 1 year." Every app shows this. None of them explain what it means for you. Sampatti does. One tap, and you see the answer — ₹5 on your ₹500 SIP, in plain language. Collapsible, inline, gone after you have read it. The information lives exactly where the question lives. Not in a separate FAQ. Not in a blog post you'll never find. Right there, when you need it, and nowhere else.</p>
  </div>

  {/* Principle 05 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 05</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">Your numbers, not generic projections</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">Every platform shows compounding calculators. Enter ₹5,000, select 10 years, see a number. That number feels like it belongs to someone else. Sampatti calculates from your income, your age, and your allocation. "₹3,000 per month starting at 23 could become ₹38 lakh by 40." That is not a marketing illustration. It is your number. When the projection is personal, the future stops being abstract. That is the moment investing stops feeling like something other people do.</p>
  </div>

  {/* Principle 06 */}
  <div className="mb-12">
    <div className="flex items-baseline gap-4 mb-3">
      <span className="text-xs font-medium tracking-wider uppercase text-amber-400">Principle 06</span>
    </div>
    <h3 className="text-2xl font-bold text-[#fafafa] mb-3">Guidance that learns, not a one-time recommendation</h3>
    <p className="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">On most platforms, the experience ends the moment you start a SIP. You get a confirmation screen, and then you are on your own. Sampatti treats the first investment as the beginning, not the end. As you invest consistently, the system adapts. Your portfolio grows with you — from first SIP to multiple goals. Recommendations evolve based on your behaviour, not a static plan you set once and forget. Other apps help you start. Sampatti helps you continue.</p>
  </div>
</section>

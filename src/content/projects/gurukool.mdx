<section class="mb-20">
  <p class="text-gray-400 text-lg leading-relaxed">
    GuruKool is an all-in-one platform designed to bridge the gap between home and school. We helped GuruKool Inc. design and build a mobile experience that empowers parents to stay involved in their children's education, students to manage their studies independently, and educators to communicate effectively with families.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="mb-20">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6">The Problem</h2>
  <div class="aspect-video w-full rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500">Problem Statement Visual</div>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed mt-6">
    Parents today juggle multiple apps, group chats, and paper notes to keep track of their children's homework and school activities. Fragmented communication between teachers and families leads to missed assignments, confusion about expectations, and unnecessary stress for both parents and students.
  </p>
  <blockquote class="border-l-4 border-white/20 pl-6 my-8 text-gray-300 italic">
    "I check three different apps every evening just to figure out what my kids need to do for homework. It's exhausting."
    <span class="block text-sm text-gray-500 mt-2 not-italic">— Parent, User Research Participant</span>
  </blockquote>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
    Key pain points identified through early stakeholder interviews:
  </p>
  <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-gray-400 mt-4 space-y-2">
    <li>No centralized place for homework tracking across multiple children and schools</li>
    <li>Teachers rely on one-way communication channels like newsletters and emails that go unread</li>
    <li>Students lack tools to develop independent study habits and time management skills</li>
    <li>Parents feel disconnected from the classroom and unsure how to help effectively</li>
    <li>Group study and peer support happen informally with no structured platform</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="mb-20">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6">Research Insights</h2>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed mb-6">
    We conducted 40+ user interviews across three personas — parents, students (ages 10–17), and teachers — along with a quantitative survey of 600 respondents. The research phase uncovered critical insights that shaped our design decisions.
  </p>
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-6">
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/5 flex items-center justify-center text-white font-bold mb-4">1</div>
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Parents Want Visibility, Not Control</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Parents don't want to micromanage homework. They want a clear view of what's due, what's completed, and where their child needs help — without hovering.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/5 flex items-center justify-center text-white font-bold mb-4">2</div>
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Students Crave Peer Support</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">78% of students said they learn best when working with peers. Study groups — even informal ones — significantly improve comprehension and motivation.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <div class="w-10 h-10 rounded-lg bg-white/5 flex items-center justify-center text-white font-bold mb-4">3</div>
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Teachers Need Two-Way Channels</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Teachers reported spending 5+ hours per week on parent communication. They wanted structured, asynchronous touchpoints rather than ad-hoc messages.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="mb-20">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6">The Solution</h2>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed mb-10">
    GuruKool brings homework management, community, and communication into a single, beautifully designed app. Here are the core features we built:
  </p>

  <div class="space-y-12">
    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">
      <div>
        <span class="text-xs font-medium text-gray-500 uppercase tracking-wider">Feature 01</span>
        <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-2 mb-3">Homework Tracker</h3>
        <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
          A unified dashboard where students see all assignments organized by subject, due date, and priority. Smart reminders nudge students before deadlines, and completion streaks gamify consistency. Parents get a read-only view with daily digest notifications.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="aspect-video w-full rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500">Homework Tracker Screen</div>
    </div>

    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">
      <div class="aspect-video w-full rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500">Study Groups Screen</div>
      <div>
        <span class="text-xs font-medium text-gray-500 uppercase tracking-wider">Feature 02</span>
        <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-2 mb-3">Study Groups</h3>
        <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
          Students can create or join study groups by subject, school, or topic. Groups include shared resource boards, a discussion thread, and a simple quiz tool so members can test each other. Moderation tools keep the space safe and focused.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">
      <div>
        <span class="text-xs font-medium text-gray-500 uppercase tracking-wider">Feature 03</span>

        <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
          A bird's-eye view of each child's academic life — upcoming assignments, grades, attendance, and teacher notes. Parents can set goals, track progress over time, and celebrate milestones. Multi-child support lets families manage everything in one place.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="aspect-video w-full rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500">Parent Dashboard Screen</div>
    </div>

    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 items-center">
      <div class="aspect-video w-full rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500">Teacher Communication Screen</div>
      <div>
        <span class="text-xs font-medium text-gray-500 uppercase tracking-wider">Feature 04</span>
        <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-2 mb-3">Teacher Communication</h3>
        <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed">
          Teachers send announcements, assignment details, and progress updates directly through the app. A templated message system saves time, while read receipts ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Parents can reply in-thread to keep conversations organized.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="mb-20">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6">Design Process</h2>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed mb-8">
    We moved through a double-diamond process — diverging during discovery and definition, then converging during development and delivery. Here's a look at how the design evolved.
  </p>

  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-6 mb-10">
    <div class="aspect-square rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500 text-sm">Low-Fi Wireframes</div>
    <div class="aspect-square rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500 text-sm">Mid-Fi Wireframes</div>
    <div class="aspect-square rounded-xl bg-gray-800 flex items-center justify-center text-gray-500 text-sm">Hi-Fi Mockups</div>
  </div>

  <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-8">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-white mb-4">Iteration Highlights</h3>
    <ul class="space-y-4 text-gray-400">
      <li class="flex gap-3">
        <span class="text-white font-bold mt-0.5">v1</span>
        <div>
          <strong class="text-white">Homework as a list.</strong> Initial designs used a simple checklist. Users found it overwhelming when they had 15+ assignments visible at once.
        </div>
      </li>
      <li class="flex gap-3">
        <span class="text-white font-bold mt-0.5">v2</span>
        <div>
          <strong class="text-white">Grouped by subject with collapsible sections.</strong> This reduced cognitive load significantly. Users could focus on one subject at a time while still having full visibility.
        </div>
      </li>
      <li class="flex gap-3">
        <span class="text-white font-bold mt-0.5">v3</span>
        <div>
          <strong class="text-white">Added a "Today" view.</strong> User testing revealed that students primarily care about what's due today and tomorrow. A prioritized daily view became the default landing screen.
        </div>
      </li>
      <li class="flex gap-3">
        <span class="text-white font-bold mt-0.5">v4</span>
        <div>
          <strong class="text-white">Streak system and micro-animations.</strong> Gamification elements increased daily active usage by 34% during beta testing.
        </div>
      </li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="mb-20">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-white mb-6">User Testing</h2>
  <p class="text-gray-400 leading-relaxed mb-6">
    We ran three rounds of usability testing with 12 participants per round, covering all three personas. Each round informed critical refinements.
  </p>
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Round 1: Navigation Confusion</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Users struggled to find the study groups feature. We moved it from a buried tab to a prominent bottom nav item, increasing discovery by 60%.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Round 2: Notification Overload</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Parents felt overwhelmed by per-assignment notifications. We introduced daily digest options and granular notification preferences.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Round 3: Onboarding Drop-off</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Students abandoned setup halfway through. We reduced onboarding to 3 screens with progressive profiling, cutting drop-off by 45%.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="border border-white/10 rounded-xl p-6">
      <h3 class="text-white font-medium mb-2">Round 3: Accessibility Gaps</h3>
      <p class="text-sm text-gray-400">Color contrast on status indicators failed WCAG AA. We redesigned the entire status system with both color and iconography for clarity.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
